Caring for Your Pet

Caring for your Dog

Dogs are known as one of the most loyal pets and man’s best friend. They do require a significant amount of care and love to thrive. It is recommended to feed them more frequently as a puppy because they require more nutrients while they’re growing. It is also recommended that you provide them with some sort of exercise daily. That could be in physical exercise such as playing in the park, or through training which exercises their mind. By putting your dog’s energy into something positive it deters them from doing something less desirable. Be sure to keep your pup happy by keeping them tick and flea free with various grooming and medication. Of course, be sure to walk them daily and enjoy your new best friend.

Caring for your Cat

Cats are regal beings known for their grace, elegance, and interesting temperament. Cats are also known for their ability to take care of themselves, but you still have an important part in their life as their owner. Pets require a litterbox to be cleaned daily and completely changed weekly to make sure that they are comfortable using it. Additionally, they require the essential amino acid taurine for their heart and eye health, so be sure to make good food choices for your new kitten. Although cats are known as relatively clean animals, you should brush them frequently to reduce shedding and the potential for hairballs. Lastly, to protect your home and furniture it is ideal to provide a space for your cat to scratch without damaging your house.

Caring for your Fish

Fish are known as a starter pet but require more than one would expect. Fish are partial to water PH and temperature, so it is imperative that you get water conditioner and a thermostat when preparing for your new fish. Additionally, be sure that the tank size is appropriate for the number of fish that you have. A general rule for tank size is 1 gallon for every inch of fish (when full grown). So, if you have a 4-inch fish you should have at a least 4-gallon tank. When getting your fish, you should leave them inside of their container and sit it inside the tank so that the water temperature can slowly adjust. This avoids shocking the fish from the sudden change in water temperature. Take care of your fish and maybe teach them a trick or two. Some can even learn to jump through hoops!

Caring for your Rabbit

Rabbits are generally very shy pets but if you treat them right, they can become your close fluffy companion. The first thing you should do is give them a safe and warm space to call home. Some rabbits can be raised as free roam in the home or inside a pen either way, their home is the most important. Second most important is to bunny proof your home. This prevents your rabbit from chewing anything that could hurt them such as wires or getting lost in vents. You also need to provide a constant supply of fresh hay as that is most of their diet. Additionally, it is good practice to set up a litterbox in the same place that they eat as they like to eat and use the bathroom at the same time. This encourages good litterbox habits. Lastly, just pay attention to your pet. Rabbits are naturally prey animals so they try to hide illnesses they may have, so keep and eye to make sure they are staying healthy. Play with your rabbits and have fun!
